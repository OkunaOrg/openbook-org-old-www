Hello world.

We're Openbook .

A new, open and secure social network coming soon to kickstarter.

Why?

The reason we're building yet another social network.

Social networks are undoubtedly one of the greatest inventions of the 21st century.

They have redefined how we connect with our loved ones, how we save and share our most precious moments, our politics, our economy, our world.

We entrust them with our stories, ideas, goals, plans and wishes. We entrust them with ourselves.

Is this trust deserved? Is the goal of these companies to help us connect with others or simply making money?

We, like you, can't tell.

Why? Because these social networks operate behind walls. Walls of uncertainty, which as shown recently, often turn out to be walls of deceit and dishonesty.

We want to bring those walls down.

We want to build an open and secure social network we can all trust.

How?

How will we create an open and secure social network?

1. Openness.

From the code running in our servers to our our T-Shirt designs, every thing we do will be free and open for everyone to see, reproduce and contribute to.

People will be able to see our intents in every line of code we write and will be able to confront us at any moment. No unpleasant surprises.

And finally, if something awful were to happen to the network ( like being overtaken by people with purely monetary interests ), with all of the code publicly available, anyone could setup their own and carry on with the mission.

2. Privacy and security.

Given our cyber security background, there is nothing we value more in a product than it's privacy and security.

We will embed privacy and security in the core of everything we do.

2.1 Data sharing with applications

One of the great benefits of having a social media account these days is being able to use it to log into other applications.

This is done through data sharing. The social network shares the information the application needs from you.

Example

Go ahead, click around.

Did you spot the problems? Let us give you hand.

Problem #1: What data did you share with the application?

Can you name the exact data that was shared with the application?

You might say, "well of course, it says clearly public profile, friend list, birthday, likes and email address".

But what is public profile really? Did you click/hover the "" icon?

When you do, you'll get some more information.

"Your public profile includes name, profile picture, age, range, gender, language, country and other public info."

Apart from wondering why in the world would a WiFi hotspot need any of these, it is still not clear what data will be shared.

This to us is unacceptable.

Problem #2: Who determines what information does the application require from you?

You might be thinking, "what a silly question, the application of course", they are providing a service, they know best.

This is the current case in every social media. You register an application, configure what information the application "needs" from your users, and you're done.

Well, in a perfect world where all humans have good intentions for others this would work.

Sadly, in our world, companies often abuse this, and with no supervision, they request and store all the information they can about you.

Be it to provide a service, analyze or sell your data. Once again, we don't know, and really, it shouldn't be our responsibility to find out.

Social media companies should be responsible for ensuring applications do not abuse data sharing systems.

All applications requesting access to user data should be closely examined, by real people, with the privacy and security of their users as their guiding principles.

Problem #3: What happens to your information once it leaves the social network?

So, you went ahead and hit Continue. What now?

Where is that data?

If you were to discover these WiFi hotspots are owned by an Evil Corporation gathering this information for massive surveillance reasons.

Could you un-athorize the data sharing? Could you delete your data from their systems?

The answer is no. Once the data is shared, there's no going back. There are no procedures for tracking or deleting it.

Our solution

These are all clear and serious problems that no social network as of today has been able to solve.

The privacy and security of their users are simply not part of their priorities.

Well, guess what? They are part of ours.

Spot the differences? Here are the most important ones.

Explicit and informed consent

Sharing our information is sharing a bit of ourselves. And that is why we want to make sure it's always crystal clear what is to be shared.

We won't hide anything behind popups, behind vague descriptions such as "public information". Simplicity and honesty all the way.

Secure applications

We will introduce the concept of secure applications.

These applications will be examined in depth by us.

We will make sure that these applications

  • Only request the information they need to provide the service.
  • Know at all times where your data is.
  • Have provable procedures in place to delete your information from ALL of their systems if you were to revoke the permission.

Goodbye data sharing abuse! Hello absolute control of your data!

2.2 No tracking by default

Although we're fans of the Do Not Track initiative. We strongly believe no "Opt-out" should be required for people not to be monitored across the internet.

Privacy should be a default everywhere.

That is why we will not track you anywhere, anyhow, by default.
